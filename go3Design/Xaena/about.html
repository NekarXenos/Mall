Introducing the Xaena Rotary Exotic Car

When you want a luxury race car for the street, you acquire an exotic. And if it’s something no-one else has, that’s even better. Most super car exotics are loosely based on some sort of race car and the all have one thing in common. They go fast, whether it be 0-60 or top speed. Mostly both. Almost every type of race car has been a basis for exotics. Except one. Rotaries. The 4-rotor 787B won the Le Mans in 1991 proving it’s reliability by traveling about 3000 miles / 5000km at 120mph / 200kph for 24 hours. Rotaries are on the race tracks all the time. Yet there has never been a street legal rotary exotic supercar. With emissions legislation getting tighter it may never happen unless we do something about it.

Aesthetically most exotics aspire to be either beautiful or look like they’re going 100 miles per hour while standing still. Or a mixture of both. If you Google images for “Most beautiful cars in the world” one of the first ten images that will attract your attention:

The Mazda Furai Concept
This was a mid-engine 3-rotor ethanol burning concept created in 2007. It was not just an empty shell like most concepts, it was driven at Laguna Seca and Goodwood. In 2008 it caught fire during a car journalist photography session at Bentwaters Parks. This was only made public in 2013.

The Mazda RX-Vision Concept
Mazda also made another beautiful concept car. In 2016 the Mazda RX-VISION sports car concept has been named ‘Most Beautiful Concept Car of the Year’ at the 31st Festival Automobile International in Paris. It featured a front mid-engine layout in Mazda’s beautiful Kodo design language.

Both these cars have sleek sexy flowing lines. While the Furai has lots of lines resembling water-flow and some alienesque features, the RX-Vision is minimalistic and is almost a single curved surface. The RX-Vision still resembles a sports car rather than a super car with its front mid-engine layout, while the Furai is halfway between a race car and a super car.

What if we mixed those two cars and gave it the same engine as the 24 hour Le Mans winning car? That is what my inspiration was for my Furai-Vision concept:
